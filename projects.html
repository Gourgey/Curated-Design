Selected projects.

A snapshot of residential, hospitality, and commercial interiors designed by the studio.

Residential

Calm, characterful homes.

Residential work focuses on spaces that feel quietly confident rather than staged – layouts that work day-to-day, materials that age well, and details that make rooms feel lived in, not showpiece.

Projects often combine tailored joinery, layered textiles, and considered lighting to create a sense of ease: bedrooms that genuinely feel restful, living rooms designed around conversation, and kitchens that actually function for how clients cook.

Selected: Garden Wall Apartment, Chelsea Townhouse, study and bedroom suites.

Hospitality

Restaurants and social spaces.

Hospitality projects are built around atmosphere: how the space feels from the threshold, the sound and light levels, and the rhythm between bar, dining, and circulation.

Schemes are tailored to the specific brand and site: layering texture, lighting, and furniture so that the space works through lunch, dinner, and late service without feeling overdesigned or theme-led.

Selected: Garden Restaurant concept, bar and lounge studies, private dining spaces.

Commercial

Workspaces and client-facing environments.

Commercial interiors balance brand expression with clear function – how teams work, meet, and host.

The focus is on clarity: routes, zones, and a material palette that can handle everyday use. From reception areas and meeting rooms to more informal lounges, the aim is to keep the design legible, calm, and aligned with the company’s identity.

Selected: concept studies for client reception, flexible meeting suites, and mixed-use spaces.
